Revisions to this Site

I asked my partner, James Price, and my co-work Brad for feedback on this site. At the time, the troop website page looked like this:

He identified the following issues:

  • The first thing that he saw was that the blue font was difficult to read with a yellow background.
  • Had a few grammar issues.
  • He indicated that the images were too small to read and unclear, would like them clearer or be able to open in a new window.
  • He thought everything centered looked odd on the page and left a lot of empty space.
  • He stated that the navigation links are lost in the background, need to make them more visible.
  • Brad suggested that I set the Girl Scouts of Middle Tennessee link to blank so that it will open in a new window.

After revising the site to account for this feedback, I ended up with the following layout:

How I used the feedback to make my website better.

  • Remove the inline style for the page. To return to the default font to match other pages.
  • With the help of Grammarly, corrected the various grammar issues.
  • Increased the width of the images, and also made them clickable to open in a new window.
  • Changed the ordered list to display: flex. Now the paragraphs are on the left and images on the right.
  • Added a background color and border to the nav content, added a border-radius. Added some padding, to help center the links.
  • I corrected the Girl Scouts of Middle Tennessee link to include a target of blank so that it will open in a new window.
